## QA-08

**Question:** What was Merlin's father, and how did it affect Merlin?

**Answer:** Merlin's father was an incubus — a supernatural being that lives between the Earth and the Moon. This heritage made Merlin a prophet from a young age and granted him the special traits of his non-human bloodline (Mixed Blood: EX).

**Source Chunk:**
> "It's said that while his mother was the princess of Wales, his father was an incubus, a supernatural being that lives between the Earth and the Moon. He was a prophet from a young age."

## QA-09

**Question:** Why is Merlin not normally summoned as a Heroic Spirit?

**Answer:** Because there is no future in which Merlin can die, he cannot be summoned as a Heroic Spirit through ordinary means. Instead, he single-handedly acquired the skill Self-Manifestation (Rank A) — normally exclusive to a certain special class — to manifest as a Servant purely as a personal indulgence, which he himself describes as a "hobby."

**Source Chunk:**
> "Merlin is not normally summoned as a Heroic Spirit. This is because there is no future where he can die. He serves his Master as a Servant to vent his selfish desires, which could essentially be called a hobby. In order to indulge this hobby of his, he single-handedly obtained this skill that only a certain special class could possess, and pretends to be summoned as a Servant."

**Source File:** `150_Merlin_lore.md` (Lines 98–100)

## QA-10

**Question:** Who are Karna's parents, and how did he obtain his Noble Phantasm Vasavi Shakti?

**Answer:** Karna is the son of Kunti, a human girl, and Surya, the God of the Sun. He obtained Vasavi Shakti — a lance of light capable of slaying gods — by surrendering his golden divine armor to the god Indra in exchange.

**Source Chunk:**
> "Karna was born of Kunti, a human girl, and Surya, the God of Sun."  
> "According to the legend, Karna gave his golden armor to Indra. In exchange for giving up and dismantling his armor, Karna received a lance powerful enough to slay even gods. It is said this lance of light cannot be wielded by even the King of Gods. When this lance is clad with crimson blood, one strike, just one strike, is said to be able to cleanse all impurities. --Vasavi Shakti."

**Source File:** `085_Karna_lore.md` (Lines 39, 60–65)
